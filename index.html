Shubhashis is a Ph.D. Researcher in Computer Science at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County (UMBC), advised by Dr. Frank Ferraro. His work bridges Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Computer Vision (CV), with a focus on Decomposition-based Reasoning ‚Äî enabling machines to reason through complex tasks by breaking them into atomic questions.

His current research explores presupposition-free question-based decomposition for assessing the scientific feasibility of tasks [1]. Previously, he investigated Outcome- and Intention-based Decomposition for video-text retrieval [2], targeting settings where videos lack textual metadata (e.g., social media or surveillance contexts). His earlier work on Hierarchical Variational Autoencoders for Event Representation Learning demonstrated strong applications in text summarization, question answering, and counterfactual reasoning [3].

In Summer 2025, he interned at Amazon (Alexa Team) as an Applied Scientist. He has shown how Deliberative Alignment technique can be used to optimize cost by ruducing token of the tool-calling LLMs (paper coming soon).

In Summer 2024, he interned at Scale AI (Enterprise Team) as a Machine Learning Researcher. He has explored how RL can for text-to-SQL generation and designing AutoEval using small-language-model reward functions (Scale AI Blog).
